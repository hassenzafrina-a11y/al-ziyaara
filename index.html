Explore Sri Lanka

Iconic Destinations

From ancient kingdoms to pristine coastlines — Sri Lanka's diverse landscape holds a lifetime of discovery.

Colombo

The Commercial Capital

Colombo is Sri Lanka's largest city and financial hub. Explore the National Museum, Natural History Museum, Dutch Period Museum, Sapumal Foundation, Lionel Wendt Art Center, and the famous Colombo Zoological Gardens.

Museums Zoos Art Shopping

Galle

The Southwestern Port City

Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit. The National Maritime Museum houses undersea artifacts. Enjoy diving at Unawatuna Bay and explore boutique shopping around the historic fort walls.

UNESCO Diving Maritime Heritage

Kandy

The Hill Capital

The royal city of Kandy sits 488m above sea level. Visit the Royal Palace, Royal Botanical Gardens at Peradeniya (home to 300+ orchid types), the Kandy Lake (built 1807), Hanthana Mountains, and the Knuckle Mountains.

Royal Palace Gardens Trekking Shopping

Anuradhapura

Ancient Capital · Est. 900 BC

One of Sri Lanka's oldest cities, Anuradhapura served as the ancient capital. Explore the Citadel, palaces, old city walls, Archaeology Museum, Folk Museum, and the legendary Lovamahapaya (Brazen Palace).

Ancient City Museums Ruins History

Polonnaruwa

World Heritage Site

A historic city that once served as Sri Lanka's capital. The entire site of Polonnaruwa is a World Heritage Site. See the ruins of the old city, the Royal Citadel, and soak in centuries of Sri Lankan history.

UNESCO Ruins Royal Citadel

National Parks

Wildlife & Nature

Sri Lanka boasts remarkable wildlife sanctuaries including Yala National Park, Kitulgala, Bundala National Park, and the breathtaking Sinharaja Rainforest. Also visit the iconic Adam's Peak pilgrimage site.

Safari Rainforest Wildlife Trekking

Coastal Paradise

Golden Beaches of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is blessed with over a thousand kilometres of palm-fringed coastline. Glistening clear waters guarantee year-round water sports, swimming, and sunbathing.

🤿 Water Sports

Snorkeling, scuba diving by professionally qualified schools, windsurfing, fishing, canoeing and water skiing — the coastal resorts offer it all.

🪸 Coral & Marine Life

Explore beautiful coral reefs and shipwrecks, large lagoons and bays with a fascinating habitat of marine life and bird life.

🏨 Accommodation

Choose from simple home stays and exclusive boutique hotels to luxurious Ayurveda spa facilities and ultra luxury 5-star hotels.

🌤 Best Time to Visit by Coast

Sri Lanka's monsoon pattern means there's always a beautiful beach to enjoy, regardless of the season.

Nov – March

Enjoy the sunny, calm beaches of the West & Southern coastline during the northeast monsoon season.

April – October

Visit the East coast during the southwest monsoon season for beautiful conditions.

A National Treasure

About Ceylon Tea

Tea production is one of the main sources of foreign exchange for Sri Lanka — formerly called Ceylon — and accounts for 2% of GDP, generating roughly $700 million annually to the economy.

It employs, directly or indirectly, over 1 million people. In 1995, it directly employed 215,338 workers on tea plantations and estates across the island.

Sri Lanka is the world's fourth largest producer of tea, and Ceylon Tea is renowned globally for its exceptional quality and unique flavour, owing to the country's diverse elevations and microclimates.

Shopping in Kandy offers some of the best Ceylon Tea bargains, alongside gems, leather products, jewellery, ceramics, and antiques — the main shopping hub being Station Road.
